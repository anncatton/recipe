Citrus Spiced Salmon - adapted from Persiana by Sabrina Ghayour

Hide Images

Salmon So Good

This is one of my favourite recipes from Sabrina Ghayour's excellent Persiana. The first time I saw this recipe I was so intrigued by the spicing - cinnamon and cumin with salmon? I had to try it immediately! It's so simple and delicious, I make it almost every week. We have a butcher nearby who sells the best salmon I've ever had, so that doesn't hurt either. I've tried it with both smaller fillets and one larger piece, and find I prefer the fish in one big piece - it requires less handling, and I like the presentation better. The larger piece requires a little longer cooking time, and if you're using a gas barbecue, keep a close eye on it! They cook a lot faster than a regular oven. I like to use less olive oil because I find salmon to be fairly fatty on its own, and the extra oil can end up pooling at the bottom of the pan. And, being me, I always throw in a little more cinnamon. I usually try to cook extra, as it's delicious cold the next day, but my family often eats it all anyway - it's just too good! Serve it with a light green salad and you're set.

Serves 6

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 15 - 35 minutes

What You'll Need

  • 4 tbsp dried edible rose petals
  • 2 tsp sumac
  • 1 tsp dried lime powder or ground kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • zest of 1 orange; grapefruit works too
  • zest of 1 lime
  • 3-6 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/2 - 2lbs salmon fillet
  • sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • lemon wedges, to serve

Process

  1. Preheat your oven to 400F/200C or fire up your barbeque, trying to get it is as close to this temperature as you can (our barbeque never gets that low, I aim for 450F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or aluminum foil. If using a barbeque, set out some foil on a pan; parchment won't survive a modern barbeque. The pan will be used to transport your salmon to and from the grill.
    2. Done this step
  2. If you have pre-ground lime powder, add that to the bowl. I haven't been able to find this in any of the nearby shops so I use dried kaffir lime leaves which I grind by hand. If you're using lime leaves, grind them in the mortar or a spice grinder. Using a mortar can be a bit challenging, and I often find I need to use my fingers to help break down the leaves. As you grind them, you'll uncover the middle stem of the leaf. Remove these, as they're a little hard to chew!
    3. Done this step
  3. Using a mortar and pestle or the spice grinder, grind your rose petals as fine as you can. I have a fairly small mortar, so I grind one half of the petals first, then the other, so I don't spill half of them on the counter. Add to your lime powder.
    4. Done this step
  4. Zest your lime and orange into a medium bowl. Measure in the cumin, cinnamon, and sumac. Give it a good stir with your measuring spoon.
    5. Done this step
  5. Now add the olive oil. Start with 3 tablespoons, mix it in and check the consistency. With this amount of oil it should feel like a rub. If you like a more oily marinade, keep adding oil one tablespoon at a time until it seems right to you, but you shouldn't need more than 6 tablespoons. Rub the marinade into all sides of the salmon fillet(s) except the skin, using a brush or your hands. Season with salt and pepper.
    6. Done this step
  6. Bake the salmon for 10-15 minutes if you have the smaller pieces, 30-35 minutes if cooking one big piece. If you're using a gas barbeque, put the foil directly on the grill and cook for a little over half that time, checking its progress periodically. (I had excellent results on the grill with a 2 1/2lb fillet for about 22 minutes at 450F.) The fish should lightly flake when turned with a fork.
    7. Done this step
